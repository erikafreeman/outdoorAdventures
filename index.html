Trip Highlights

Route Selection: Mix of classic alpine routes including Aiguille du Midi traverse, Cosmiques Arête, and the Vallée Blanche glacier tour. Perfect introduction to high-altitude alpine climbing with spectacular views throughout.

Conditions: Exceptional weather window with stable high pressure, minimal wind, and clear skies for 10 of 14 days. Early season snowfall provided excellent névé conditions on glaciated approaches.

Race Strategy

Pacing Plan: Conservative first 50 miles maintaining conversational effort, focusing on nutrition every 30 minutes. Planned to negative split if feeling strong after mile 62 aid station.

Execution: Stuck to plan through mile 70, then hit the wall hard during night section. Mental game became crucial - broke remaining distance into 5-mile segments with specific goals.

Nutrition Strategy

Combination of real food and sports nutrition. Focused on 200-300 calories per hour with balanced carbs, electrolytes, and caffeine management for night running.

Fueling Breakdown:

  • Hours 0-6: Sandwiches, bananas, electrolyte drink
  • Hours 6-12: Gels, salt tabs, diluted Coke
  • Hours 12-20: Soup, caffeine, whatever stays down
  • Hours 20+: Survival mode - Coke and determination

Cultural Immersion

Stayed in estancias (traditional ranches), learned about gaucho culture, and experienced authentic Patagonian asado. Language barrier created opportunity for creative communication and deeper cultural exchange.

Local Experiences:

  • Estancia Cristina sheep shearing demonstration
  • Traditional asado with local family in El Calafate
  • Horseback riding with gaucho guide
  • Local mate tea ceremony and traditions
Budget Breakdown

Combination of camping, hostels, and occasional splurge accommodations. Cooking own meals when possible, local restaurants for cultural experiences. Bus transport between major destinations.
